Sustainability

Sustainability on foods offered at Vj'z



Vegan Burgers

Consuming beef is very environmentally taxing (beef consumes 112 liters of water per gram of protein, and uses 119.49 of land per 1000 kilocalories) , while veggie meat patties are a more sustainable alternative and are overall better environmentally. Vegan patties are a better alternative compared to the common beef that is put in burgers.

Bean Burgers

Beans are an excellent source of plant-based protein, which make them an fantastic choice for vegetarians, vegans, and those looking to reduce their meat consumption. Also, beans have a lot of fiber which is needed to maintain healthy digestion and to prevent constipation.

Mushroom Soup

The amount of water required to make a pound of mushrooms is only a fraction of water compared to many other foods. Also, since mushrooms are very small in size, mushrooms are able to be grown in high volumes with very little space. These facts proves how mushrooms have good sustainability.

Rice and Vegan Stew

Farming rice has the lowest soil erosion on a per-acre basis compared to other crops. Also, rice improves on-farm profitability, supports local communities, and significantly contributes to the U.S. economy.
Vegan stew has very low calorie density, which mean that the calories you get from an ounce is very low. Also vegan stew is a very good source of fiber for people.

Fruit Salad

Foods like Strawberries and Blackberries are good in the sustainability department. For strawberries, farmers should look for well-drained and sandy soils, since these are soil environments where strawberries are grown best. Blackberries have very low carbon footprint because they usually use manual harvesting and processing practices.

Salad

Salads are often high in fiber, which means that they can help stop constipation, they can regulate your cholesterol levels, and help you lose weight. Salads also contain good fats, salads include monosaturated or polyunsaturated fats which can boost your heart and brain health.
